--- paper_title: A Machine Learning Pipeline to Predict Vegetation Health abstract: Agricultural droughts can exacerbate poverty and lead to famine. Timely distribution of disaster relief funds is essential to help minimise the impact of drought. Indices of vegetation health are indicative of higher risk of agricultural drought, but their prediction remains challenging, particularly in Africa. Here, we present an open-source machine learning pipeline for climate-related data. Specifically, we train and analyse a recurrent model to predict pixel-wise vegetation health in Kenya. q1_track: Papers primary_contact_author_name: Thomas Lees primary_contact_author_email: thomas.lees@chch.ox.ac.uk status: Accept (spotlight) primary_subject_area: Disaster management and relief secondary_subject_areas: Extreme weather events author_names: Lees, Thomas*; Tseng, Gabriel; Dadson, Simon; Hernández, Alex; Atzberger, Clement G.; Reece, Steven author_emails: thomas.lees@chch.ox.ac.uk*; gabrieltseng95@gmail.com; simon.dadson@ouce.ox.ac.uk; alexhg15@gmail.com; clement.atzberger@boku.ac.at; reece@robots.ox.ac.uk authors: Thomas Lees (University of Oxford); Gabriel Tseng (Okra Solar); Simon Dadson (University of Oxford); Alex Hernández (University of Osnabrück); Clement G.
